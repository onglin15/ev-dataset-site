Lab Introduction

The Intelligent Transportation and EV Data Lab studies data-driven mobility systems through field experiments, sensing infrastructure, and interpretable modeling. Our work connects transportation engineering, intelligent vehicles, and machine learning to support reproducible academic research.

We focus on building reliable datasets and practical methods that can be used by researchers working on EV operation, driving intelligence, traffic analysis, and sustainable urban mobility.

Research Focus

  • Intelligent transportation and scenario-aware traffic analysis
  • Autonomous driving data collection with synchronized sensors
  • EV energy consumption, charging, and route-dependent efficiency
  • Multimodal sensing and data fusion for mobility intelligence

Research Directions

Core topics and methodology

01

Intelligent Transportation

We study mobility dynamics, traffic scenarios, and vehicle operations to support data-driven intelligent transportation planning and system analysis.

02

Autonomous Driving Data Collection

Our group develops synchronized data collection workflows that combine cameras, GPS, vehicle telemetry, and contextual annotations in real urban driving environments.

03

EV Energy Analysis

We analyze battery state, energy consumption, charging behavior, and route-aware efficiency to understand practical performance in electric mobility systems.

04

Multimodal Sensing and Data Fusion

We integrate heterogeneous sensing streams to improve scenario understanding, model robustness, and downstream mobility intelligence.

Dataset

PAVE Dataset

Production Autonomous Vehicle Evaluation

Overview

PAVE is a real-world end-to-end dataset designed for production autonomous vehicle evaluation. The current release provides a selected academic subset for non-commercial research with synchronized sensors and high-precision localization.

Compared with conventional perception-oriented datasets, PAVE is positioned for system-level evaluation of autonomous driving behavior, safety, and trajectory quality under identified autonomous driving mode.

Open Dataset Page GitHub Repository

Release Snapshot

  • Autonomous-mode road logs
  • Synchronized image and video streams
  • GNSS / IMU with high-precision localization
  • Calibration files and benchmark-oriented packaging
01

Highlights

Research-oriented release for safety analysis, behavior understanding, reliability evaluation, and future benchmark development.

02

Sensor Setup

Includes front and side cameras, GNSS / IMU, and synchronization support for cross-modal analysis and trajectory reconstruction.

03

Access

Released as an academic non-commercial subset with formal citation requirements and separate contact for full-data or commercial licensing.

Included Modalities

  • Image sequences for visual analysis
  • Video streams for session-level evaluation
  • GNSS / IMU logs for localization and temporal alignment
  • Calibration files for reproducible processing
  • Safety-oriented material for behavior analysis

Access and Citation

The public release is intended for academic research, scientific investigation, and non-commercial benchmarking. Proper citation of the dataset paper is required for any research output using the data.

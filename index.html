Intelligent Transportation Research Group

We build open, structured datasets for electric vehicle analysis, intelligent driving research, and multimodal transportation understanding.

Our group integrates vehicle telemetry, environmental context, and real-world mobility scenarios to support reproducible academic research.

Dataset Focus

  • EV energy consumption and SOC behavior
  • Multimodal sensing across cameras, GPS, and CAN/OBD
  • Scenario-aware driving data for downstream modeling

Research

Core Directions

Our work combines transportation systems, data infrastructure, and machine learning to create high-quality benchmarks for electric mobility research.

01

EV Dataset Construction

Curating synchronized vehicle, route, and operational records for robust benchmarking and open scientific use.

02

Energy and Range Modeling

Studying battery usage, driving behavior, and environment-aware energy prediction for electric vehicles.

03

Intelligent Driving Analytics

Mining multimodal driving data to understand scenarios, decision patterns, and safety-relevant events.

04

Urban Mobility Sensing

Integrating spatial, temporal, and vehicle-side observations to support data-driven transportation research in real environments.
